Mid-Autumn Festival

The story of Hou Yi and Chang’e plays a central role in the Mid-Autumn Festival, one of the most important traditional festivals in China. Celebrated on the 15th day of the 8th month of the lunar calendar, the festival is marked by the full moon, symbolizing reunion and harmony. During the festival, people gather with family and friends to admire the full moon, light lanterns, and enjoy mooncakes, a special pastry often filled with sweet or savory ingredients. The tradition of offering mooncakes to Chang’e originates from the story where Hou Yi, after Chang’e ascended to the moon, would leave her favorite desserts as a sign of forgiveness. This act of honoring Chang’e continues today, as offerings are made to the moon goddess during the festival.

Influence in Chinese Art and Literature

The story of Hou Yi and Chang’e has been a rich source of inspiration for Chinese art and literature. Traditional paintings, calligraphy, and poetry often depict scenes of Hou Yi shooting down the suns or Chang’e’s ascent to the moon. In literature, the myth is referenced in classical texts like the "Heavenly Questions" and has been adapted into countless folktales and plays. The imagery of the archer and the moon goddess has also influenced contemporary art forms, appearing in modern illustrations and digital media, reflecting the enduring legacy of these mythological figures in Chinese culture.

Representation in Popular Culture

Hou Yi and Chang’e continue to influence popular culture today. They have been featured in numerous movies, TV dramas, and animated series, including the animated film "Over the Moon," which tells a modern retelling of Chang’e’s story. Representations of Hou Yi also appears in video games, such as "SMITE" and "Nine Sols."" The tale of Hou Yi and Chang’e is often used as a symbol of love, sacrifice, and the longing for reunion, resonating with audiences worldwide. These modern adaptations keep the ancient story alive, introducing new generations to these iconic figures of Chinese mythology.
